About Portuga

Portuga, originally a nickname given to Portuguese immigrants around the world, opened its doors as a premier San Francisco dining establishment in 2004. A unique tribute to the rich & varied regional tapa-style delicacies of Portugal. From spicey to sweet, fine sea food to street food, we promise you've never had anything like it.

MENU


Seasonal menu of tapas that give you all the [GOOD] feels.



  • Leitão À Bairrada

    Spit-Roasted Pork 'A la' Bairrada, delicately marinated in garlic and pepper paste. Crisp skin and moist meat douzed in fresh squeezed orange. Served with homemade potato chips and orange slices.


  • Francesinha Do Porto

    Oporto Francesinha Sandwich' Layered bread, linguiça, fresh sausage, steak and ham. Topped with fried egg, melted cheese, tomato,shrimp & beer sauce. Served with french fries and side salad.


  • Alheira Assada

    Savory, richly flavored white & game-meat smoked sausage with hint of sweetness. Grilled and served with toasted Portuguese Rustic bread, sliced tomato and olive oil. Recommended pairing: Vinho Verde.


  • Porco À Alentejana

    Pork 'A La' Alentejana: Twice-cooked paprika-infused potatoes with pork, clam & mussels marinated in garlic, butter, parsley and white wine sauce.


  • Bolinhos de Bacalhau d'Avó

    Grandma's Fried Codish Cakes, a traditional tapa made with salted codfish, potatoes, parsley, onions, garlic and green onions with a hint of pepper.


  • Salada de Polvo

    Grilled Calamari Salad - Grilled calamari on a bed of greens, shaved carrots, red onions, radish, parsley, red pepper flakes and sauteed potatoes. Served with toasted Portuguese bread, Portuguese Olive Oil and Vinegar.


  • Rissóis

    Portuguese Dumplings. Crisp bread crumb-covered outter layer, soft doughy inner layer. Filling types: garlic and onion cooked ground beef, shrimp fondue or veggie. Served warm with small, fresh tomato salad. Recommended pairing: Bairrada Red wine or Vinho Verde.


  • Sopa de Castanhas

    Chestnut Soup. Thick, rich veggie stock soup with pureed chestnut, chunks of Chouriço and Linguiça sausages. Topped with grilled onions and fresh parsley.


  • Prego No prato

    Thinly layered beef, cooked stove-top in garlic, onion, olive oil and bay leaf. Topped with grilled onions and red bell peppers. Served on toasted Portuguese Bread roll. Served with fresh-cut fries & side salad. Recommended pairing: Vinho Branco Minhoto
