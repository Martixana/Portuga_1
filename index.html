About Portuga

'Portuga', originally a nickname given to Portuguese immigrants in Brazil, opened its doors, as a premier San Francisco dining establishment in 2004. A unique tribute to the rich & varied regional tapa-style delicacies of Portugal. From spicey to sweet, fine sea food to street food, we promise you've never had anything like it.

'Porco À Alentejana' Pork 'A La' Alentejana is a pork and mussels dish, marinated in garlic and white wine.

'Bolinhos de Bacalhau da Vovó' Grandma's Fried Codish Cakes, a traditional tapa made from Salted Cod and potatoes

Leitão À Bairrada Spit-Roasted Pork 'A la' Bairrada, delicately marinated in garlic and pepper paste. Crisp skin douzed in fresh-squeezed orange.

'Francesinha' Made with bread, wet-cured ham, linguiça, fresh sausage, steak and ham. Covered with melted cheese, thick tomato and beer sauce, and served with french fries.

'Alheira' Richly flavored savory white-meat smoked sausage with a hint of sweetness. Served with toasted bread and olive oil
